CRI Construction Details

The Campus Readiness Index (CRI) is constructed from six indicators across three theoretical dimensions, with each indicator designed to capture a distinct aspect of organizational capacity

Experience Dimension (Factor 1)
Captures organizational memory and institutional knowledge

Theoretical Foundation
Operationalizes institutional memory through teacher experience patterns. When experienced educators constitute a majority within a campus, embodied knowledge and routines enable consistent reproduction of effective practices and transmission to inexperienced staff, creating organizational stability and instructional coherence.
Teacher Experience Concentration
Measures the proportion of teachers with substantial teaching experience (≥5 years). This threshold reflects research showing teachers experience their most substantial effectiveness gains during the first 3-5 years of their careers.
Scoring: 0-2 points
2 pts: ≥85% experienced teachers
1 pt: 70-84.99% experienced teachers
0 pts: <70% experienced teachers
Teacher Experience Distribution
Assesses equity in access to experienced teachers across the organization. Captures whether schools successfully avoid concentrating inexperienced teachers while ensuring equitable distribution of experienced staff.
Scoring: 0-1 points
1 pt: % inexperienced teachers below district average
0 pts: % inexperienced teachers at/above district average

Theoretical Foundation
Operationalizes normative isomorphism through professional credentials that serve as both technical qualifications and legitimized symbols enhancing organizational standing. Professional standardization creates institutional mechanisms that signal competence and maintain legitimacy within the educational field.
Teacher Credential Quality
Assesses the proportion of teachers with full professional credentials. Combines degree status with salary differentials, as uncertified teachers typically command lower starting salaries and can be identified through compensation patterns.
Scoring: 0-2 points
2 pts: ≥95% teachers with full credentials
1 pt: 90-94.99% teachers with full credentials
0 pts: <90% teachers with full credentials
Teacher Credential Distribution
Examines equity in allocation of credentialed staff across the organization. Ensures schools maintain high aggregate credential levels while distributing credentialed teachers equitably rather than concentrating them in particular areas.
Scoring: 0-1 points
1 pt: % credentialed teachers exceeds district average
0 pts: % credentialed teachers at/below district average

Theoretical Foundation
Captures persistence and routinization within institutional theory by examining how leadership continuity and staff retention create conditions for standardization of effective practices. Stability enables trust development, organizational coherence, and embedded improvement initiatives.
Principal Tenure
Captures leadership stability through tenure at current campus and total administrative experience. Constructed through longitudinal analysis of TAPR data, identifying transitions through changes in reported experience patterns.
Scoring: 0-2 points
2 pts: 3+ years at campus OR 8+ years total experience
1 pt: 2 years at campus OR 5-7 years total experience
0 pts: <2 years at campus AND <5 years total experience
Teacher Retention
Binary measure using longitudinal analysis to identify unusual deviations in average teacher experience suggesting higher-than-normal turnover. Normal changes range ±0.5 years; threshold of 0.75 years identifies atypical patterns.
Scoring: 0-1 points
1 pt: Year-to-year experience change <0.75 years (stable)
0 pts: Year-to-year experience change ≥0.75 years (high turnover)

Data

Data Sources

This study utilizes publicly available data from the Texas Education Agency (TEA) databases spanning seven academic years (2017-2023).

  • Texas Academic Performance Reports (TAPR): Comprehensive school-level information on staff characteristics, student demographics, and academic performance
  • Texas Education Directory (AskTED): Organizational information on school locations, regions, counties, urbanicity classifications, and charter status
  • Final Dataset: 48,620 school-year observations from public schools across Texas after applying exclusion criteria

Sample Coverage

Comprehensive representation across diverse school contexts in Texas

  • Schools: 8,605 unique campus observations
  • Time Period: 2017-2023 (7 academic years)
  • Geographic Coverage: All 20 Education Service Center regions
  • School Types: Urban, suburban, rural, and town contexts
  • Exclusions: Alternative campuses and schools with incomplete data

Data Quality

Data cleaning and validation procedures ensure analytical reliability

  • Treatment of missing data and outlier detection
  • Year-over-year validation checks for consistency
  • Longitudinal analysis for principal tenure identification
  • Cross-validation of administrative records

Key Variables

Outcomes

Student Achievement

  • Mathematics & Reading Language Arts Proficiency: Percentage of students meeting grade-level standards on STAAR assessments
  • Rationale: "Meets" standard represents federal ESSA expectations and TEA's primary academic benchmark for grade-level mastery

Key Predictors

CRI Measures

  • Individual Indicators: Six component measures
  • Factor Scores: F1 (Experience), F2 (Credentials), F3 (Stability)
  • Latent Class Profiles: Low, Typical, & High Capacity
  • Categorical Levels: Minimal (0-49), Moderate (50-69), Optimal (70-100)

Control Variables

Socioeconomic Controls

Standardized percentage of economically disadvantaged students per campus to account for socioeconomic effects on both organizational capacity and achievement

Fixed Effects

Year fixed effects for temporal trends and policy changes; District fixed effects for unobserved district characteristics that influence both capacity and outcomes

Research Questions & Analytical Approach

1 Dimensional Structure

Do distinct dimensions of organizational capacity emerge from administrative indicators available in Texas public schools?

Analytical Approach: Correlation Analysis

Correlation analysis examines internal structure of six CRI indicators to identify meaningful patterns of association corresponding to three theoretical dimensions. Item-total and item-rest correlations assess coherence of the composite measure while examining within-dimension versus cross-dimension relationships for discriminant validity.

2 Construct Validity

Does the Campus Readiness Index demonstrate structural validity using complementary analytical approaches?

Analytical Approach: Factor Analysis + Latent Class Analysis

Factor analysis with varimax rotation validates theoretical structure, examining KMO adequacy (>0.50), factor loadings with minimal cross-loadings, and eigenvalues supporting three-factor solution. Latent class analysis serves as robustness check, identifying naturally occurring organizational profiles through cross-tabulation with CRI readiness levels.

3 Criterion Validity

What is the relationship between organizational capacity measures and student achievement outcomes?

Analytical Approach: Fixed-Effects Regression Models

Fixed-effects regression models isolate capacity-achievement relationships across multiple specifications (individual indicators, factor scores, latent class profiles, CRI categories). Staged approach begins with year fixed effects for state-wide comparisons, then introduces district fixed effects for within-district comparisons, examining both mathematics and reading outcomes.

Empirical Strategy for Research Question 3

Year Fixed Effects Model

Yit = β0 + β1ORGit + β2Xit + δt + εit

Provides upper bound estimates by comparing schools across the entire state, capturing full range of capacity variation

District Fixed Effects Model (Preferred Specification)

Yit = β0 + β1ORGit + β2Xit + δt + δd + εit

Provides conservative estimates by comparing schools within same district, controlling for district-level policies and contexts

Model Terms

Yit: Achievement outcome (mathematics or reading language arts) for school i in year t

ORGit: CRI capacity measure (individual indicators, factor scores, latent class profiles, CRI categories)

Xit: Standardized percentage of economically disadvantaged students

δt: Year fixed effects (policy changes, temporal trends)

δd: District fixed effects (unobserved district characteristics)

εit: Error term, with standard errors clustered at district level

Identification Strategy

District Fixed Effects: Control for unobserved district-level factors including resource allocation, leadership approaches, and policy environments

Year Fixed Effects: Account for state-wide policy changes, testing variations, and temporal trends

Clustered Standard Errors: Address within-district correlation in outcomes and organizational practices

Multiple Specifications: Test robustness across different operationalizations of organizational capacity

Key Assumptions & Interpretation

Conditional Correlation: Results represent conditional associations between organizational capacity and achievement, controlling for observed district and temporal factors. Causal interpretation requires assumption that unobserved school-level factors are not systematically related to both capacity and outcomes.

Within-District Variation: District fixed effects model exploits variation in organizational capacity across schools within the same district, providing more conservative estimates by controlling for district-level confounds.

Staged Approach: Comparison between year-only and district fixed effects models helps assess sensitivity to different levels of controls and identifies potential confounding from district-level factors.

Scalable measure of organizational capacity using only administrative data, enabling system-wide assessment without surveys or observations.

  • Proxy measures demonstrate strong validity
  • Annual updates without additional costs

Key Validation Insights

Experience is King

Teacher experience concentration emerges as the strongest predictor across all specifications, validating institutional theory's emphasis on organizational memory. Strategic staffing beats spreading experienced teachers thin across all schools.

Math vs. Reading Sensitivity

Organizational capacity shows consistently stronger effects on mathematics than reading achievement, suggesting math instruction benefits more from organizational coherence and coordinated instructional practices.

District Context Matters

Effects remain significant even with district fixed effects, proving organizational capacity differences matter among schools operating under similar district policies and resource constraints. The conservative estimates strengthen causal claims.

Multiple Measures, Same Story

Convergent validity across four different analytical approaches (individual indicators, factors, latent classes, categorical levels) demonstrates the robustness of the CRI framework and strengthens confidence in the measure.

Takeaway: The CRI captures meaningful organizational differences that consistently predict student achievement across multiple analytical approaches. Experience concentration emerges as the critical factor, with effects persisting even when comparing schools within the same district. These findings validate both the theoretical framework and practical utility of the measure.

Theoretical Contributions

Limitations

  • Some indicators rely on administrative proxies (e.g., salary differentials for certification status) rather than direct measures
  • Longitudinal analysis of experience patterns provides indirect measure of turnover patterns
  • Bridges gap between organizational theory and practical measurement
  • Cross-sectional nature limits causal claims about capacity-achievement relationships
