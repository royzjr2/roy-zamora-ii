CRI Construction Details

The Campus Readiness Index (CRI) is constructed from six indicators across three theoretical dimensions, with each indicator designed to capture a distinct aspect of organizational capacity

Experience Dimension (Factor 1)
Captures organizational memory and institutional knowledge
25.6% variance
Loading: 0.87

Theoretical Foundation
Operationalizes institutional memory through teacher experience patterns. When experienced educators constitute a majority within a campus, embodied knowledge and routines enable consistent reproduction of effective practices and transmission to inexperienced staff, creating organizational stability and instructional coherence.
Teacher Experience Concentration
Measures the proportion of teachers with substantial teaching experience (≥5 years). This threshold reflects research showing teachers experience their most substantial effectiveness gains during the first 3-5 years of their careers.
Scoring: 0-2 points
2 pts: ≥85% experienced teachers
1 pt: 70-84.99% experienced teachers
0 pts: <70% experienced teachers
Teacher Experience Distribution
Assesses equity in access to experienced teachers across the organization. Captures whether schools successfully avoid concentrating inexperienced teachers while ensuring equitable distribution of experienced staff.
Scoring: 0-1 points
1 pt: % inexperienced teachers below district average
0 pts: % inexperienced teachers at/above district average
Credential Dimension (Factor 2)
Reflects normative isomorphism through professional standardization
19.0% variance
Loading: 0.68
▼
Theoretical Foundation
Operationalizes normative isomorphism through professional credentials that serve as both technical qualifications and legitimized symbols enhancing organizational standing. Professional standardization creates institutional mechanisms that signal competence and maintain legitimacy within the educational field.
Teacher Credential Quality
Assesses the proportion of teachers with full professional credentials. Combines degree status with salary differentials, as uncertified teachers typically command lower starting salaries and can be identified through compensation patterns.
Scoring: 0-2 points
2 pts: ≥95% teachers with full credentials
1 pt: 90-94.99% teachers with full credentials
0 pts: <90% teachers with full credentials
Teacher Credential Distribution
Examines equity in allocation of credentialed staff across the organization. Ensures schools maintain high aggregate credential levels while distributing credentialed teachers equitably rather than concentrating them in particular areas.
Scoring: 0-1 points
1 pt: % credentialed teachers exceeds district average
0 pts: % credentialed teachers at/below district average
Organizational Stability Dimension (Factor 3)
Captures persistence and routinization through continuity patterns
16.8% variance
Loading: 0.78
▼
Theoretical Foundation
Captures persistence and routinization within institutional theory by examining how leadership continuity and staff retention create conditions for standardization of effective practices. Stability enables trust development, organizational coherence, and embedded improvement initiatives.
Principal Tenure
Captures leadership stability through tenure at current campus and total administrative experience. Constructed through longitudinal analysis of TAPR data, identifying transitions through changes in reported experience patterns.
Scoring: 0-2 points
2 pts: 3+ years at campus OR 8+ years total experience
1 pt: 2 years at campus OR 5-7 years total experience
0 pts: <2 years at campus AND <5 years total experience
Teacher Retention
Binary measure using longitudinal analysis to identify unusual deviations in average teacher experience suggesting higher-than-normal turnover. Normal changes range ±0.5 years; threshold of 0.75 years identifies atypical patterns.
Scoring: 0-1 points
1 pt: Year-to-year experience change <0.75 years (stable)
0 pts: Year-to-year experience change ≥0.75 years (high turnover)

Research Questions

1 Dimensional Structure

Do distinct dimensions of organizational capacity emerge from administrative indicators available in Texas public schools?

Hypothesis: Six indicators will form coherent dimensions with meaningful within-dimension correlations stronger than cross-dimension relationships, supporting a multidimensional organizational capacity construct.

2 Construct Validity

Does the Campus Readiness Index demonstrate internal consistency and structural validity using complementary analytical approaches?

Hypothesis: Factor analysis will yield a three-dimensional structure (Experience, Credentials, Stability) with clear separation and minimal cross-loadings. Latent class analysis will identify organizational profiles corresponding to CRI readiness levels.

3 Criterion Validity

Is organizational capacity associated with student achievement outcomes?

Hypothesis: Higher CRI levels will demonstrate positive associations with student achievement in mathematics and reading/language arts, with relationships persisting across multiple specifications (individual indicators, factor scores, latent classes, and readiness levels).

Institutional Theory Foundation

Organizations develop capacity through standardized structures and practices that become embedded within the institution.

  • Normative isomorphism through credentials
  • Organizational memory via experience
  • Routinization through stability

Three-Factor Structure

Factor analysis reveals three distinct dimensions explaining 61.5% of organizational capacity variance.

  • Experience Concentration (25.6%)
  • Credential Quality (19.0%)
  • Organizational Stability (16.8%)

Practical Application

CRI enables systematic assessment of organizational capacity using readily available administrative data.

  • Scalable across 8,605 campuses
  • 7 years of longitudinal data
  • 48,620 campus-year observations

Measurement Innovation & Future Directions

Measurement Innovation

Scalable measure of organizational capacity using only administrative data, enabling system-wide assessment without surveys or observations.

  • Categorical scoring captures meaningful thresholds
  • Proxy measures demonstrate strong validity
  • Annual updates without additional costs

Differential Estimates by Dimension

Experience dimension shows strongest achievement associations, highlighting importance of institutional knowledge and expertise.

  • Experience Factor: 1.67pp math, 1.37pp reading
  • Credentials Factor: 1.30pp math, 0.69pp reading
  • Stability Factor: 0.22pp math, 0.12pp reading

Future Research Opportunities

CRI enables new research directions previously constrained by measurement limitations.

  • Longitudinal capacity development trajectories
  • Policy impact on organizational functioning
  • Contextual moderation of capacity associations
  • Mechanisms linking capacity to outcomes

Key Research Findings

Correlation Analysis

Meaningful patterns of association among indicators confirm theoretical clustering aligned with organizational capacity dimensions.

Factor Analysis

Three-dimensional structure with clear separation, minimal cross-loadings, and KMO = 0.5176 confirms construct validity.

Latent Class Analysis

Identifies naturally occurring organizational profiles corresponding to CRI readiness categories (χ² = 24,305.22, p < 0.001).

Criterion Validity

Significant relationships with student achievement: 4.67pp gain in math, 3.39pp gain in reading for optimal schools.
