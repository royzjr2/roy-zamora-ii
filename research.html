Research Overview

Understanding how school organizational capacity can be measured, why it matters for equity, and how policies can influence it.

“Can school organizational capacity be validly measured using administrative data, does it relate to student outcomes across diverse populations, and to what extent do performance-based policies affect these organizational structures?”

The novelty: this study addresses both measurement and mechanism questions by developing the Campus Capacity Index (CCI) and using it to examine associations across different institutional contexts and how the Teacher Incentive Allotment (TIA) transforms school organizations.

Critical literature gaps addressed

  • Measurement gap: limited validated measures of organizational capacity exist using readily available administrative data
  • Mechanism gap: how performance-based compensation works through organizational structures remains unclear
  • Equity gap: differential effects across student populations and institutional contexts are underexplored
  • Temporal gap: long-term organizational development processes vs. immediate policy effects

Three primary research questions

  • RQ1: To what extent can school organizational capacity be validly and reliably measured through a composite index using readily available Texas administrative data?
  • RQ2: Does school organizational capacity, as measured by the CCI, relate to both immediate and long-term student outcomes across diverse school contexts and student populations?
  • RQ3: Does the initial approval of a performance-based compensation policy (Texas Teacher Incentive Allotment) affect schools' campus capacity?

🔍 Validation strategies

  • Correlation analysis
    Confirms meaningful structure within and across capacity dimensions
  • Factor analysis
    Establishes construct validity through a clean, theory-aligned structure
  • Latent class analysis
    Reveals empirical configurations consistent with CCI categories
  • Criterion validity testing
    Demonstrates predictive value of the CCI for achievement outcomes

🔬 Analytical techniques

  • Fixed effects regressions
    Control for unobserved heterogeneity
  • Staggered difference-in-differences
    Exploits variation in policy rollout across districts to estimate causal effects
  • Event study framework
    Tests pre-trends and dynamic treatment effects
  • Bias-corrected estimators
    Addresses concerns with heterogeneous treatment timing
  • Causal mediation within DiD
    Traces indirect policy effects via organizational capacity over time

Finding 04 · Policy impact analysis

Performance-based pay can build organizational capacity

TIA effects on campus capacity over time (event study)

Effect sizes in standard deviations. * Year 3 overall effect statistically significant; Experience dimension p < 0.001 by Year 4.

Dimensional insights

  • Experience dimension: strongest systematic response (0.22 SD by Year 4, p < 0.001) — districts compete more effectively for experienced teachers
  • Stability dimension: initial attraction effect (0.20 SD Year 1), then mixed patterns
  • Credential dimension: no systematic improvement — suggests a shift toward performance-based hiring over credentials

Key insight

Organizational development requires 2–3 years and operates through strategic teacher recruitment rather than retention or credentialing.

Key insight

TIA operates primarily through CCI development rather than direct achievement effects — supporting organizational patience over quick fixes.

Early takeaways

Early results demonstrate that the Campus Capacity Index successfully captures organizational differences associated with student outcomes, with particularly strong benefits for historically marginalized populations. While organizational capacity develops gradually over 2–3 years rather than immediately, the CCI provides a validated tool for identifying schools needing support and tracking long-term organizational development across diverse contexts.

Policy Implications

Actionable insights for policymakers, district leaders, researchers, and practitioners.

🎯 For policymakers

  • Focus on organizational development: performance-based compensation works through strengthening organizations, not just individual incentives
  • Allow implementation time: organizational changes require 2–3 years to show full effects
  • Context-sensitive design: traditional public schools may be better positioned than charter schools
  • Equity mechanism: well-implemented policies provide the greatest benefits to marginalized students

🏫 For district leaders

  • Invest in organizational capacity: prioritize experience distribution, credential quality, and leadership stability
  • Strategic staffing: concentrate experienced teachers strategically rather than spreading thin
  • Leadership continuity: principal stability is crucial for organizational development
  • Data-driven assessment: use the CCI framework to assess and improve organizational capacity

🔬 For researchers

  • Mechanism-focused research: examine how policies work through organizational structures
  • Mediating pathways: investigate policy transmission mechanisms
  • Contextual variation: study how institutional contexts moderate policy effectiveness
  • Replication opportunities: adapt the CCI framework to other states and contexts

📊 For practitioners

  • Organizational assessment: regularly evaluate school capacity using validated frameworks
  • Targeted interventions: address specific capacity dimensions based on school needs
  • Professional development: invest in building organizational coherence and stability
  • Equity focus: high-capacity environments particularly benefit vulnerable populations
