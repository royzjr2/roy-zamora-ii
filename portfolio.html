Current Research & Focus

The Campus Readiness Index: A Novel Approach to Measuring School Organizational Capacity

Research Focus: A three-paper investigation that develops the Campus Readiness Index (CRI) to measure school organizational capacity, examines how capacity affects diverse student populations and long-term outcomes, and evaluates how performance-based policies influence organizational structures. Using seven years of Texas data (2017-2023, n=48,620 observations), this research integrates institutional and ecological systems theories to understand organizational mechanisms in education.

Methodology: Factor analysis and latent class modeling to develop the CRI, fixed effects regression and interaction models to examine equity implications, and event study design with mediation analysis to evaluate policy impacts. Integrates difference-in-differences estimation, regional variation analysis, and formal mediation testing across multiple analytical approaches.

Key Findings: High-capacity schools produce 4.67 percentage-point achievement advantages, with marginalized students benefiting most (6+ percentage-point gains for emergent bilinguals). While performance-based compensation policies produce modest organizational capacity gains (0.032 SD per year), schools that improve their CRI demonstrate substantial achievement increases of 1.98 percentage points in mathematics and 1.41 percentage points in reading — highlighting organizational capacity as the critical pathway for student success.
