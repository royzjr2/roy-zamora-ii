Current Research & Focus

The Campus Readiness Index: A Novel Approach to Measuring School Organizational Capacity

Research Focus: Developing and validating a Campus Readiness Index (CRI) that quantifies schools' organizational capacity using readily available administrative data, drawing on institutional theory and analyzing seven years of Texas administrative data (2017-2023, n=48,620 campus-year observations).

Methodology: Employing factor analysis to examine six indicators of organizational readiness including teacher experience, credentials, principal tenure, and resource distribution. Using fixed effects regression models and difference-in-differences approaches to validate against student outcomes and examine policy impacts.

Key Finding: Preliminary results reveal three distinct dimensions of campus readiness with statistically significant relationships to student achievement - a one-level increase in campus readiness grade associates with 3.20 percentage-point increases in mathematics proficiency and 2.87 percentage-point increases in reading proficiency.
